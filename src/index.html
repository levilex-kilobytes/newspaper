Students Learns HTML

HTML (HyperText Markup Language) is the fundamental, standardized language used to create and structure web pages, making it the essential first step for students learning web development.

When learning HTML, students move beyond simply typing text to using code to organize information, ensuring content is accessible, semantic, and well-structured. This foundation enables them to create static websites that can be rendered on any device, setting the stage for adding design with CSS and interactivity with JavaScript.

CSS is Apparently a Thing

Cascading Style Sheets (CSS) is the fundamental stylesheet language used to define the layout, colors, fonts, and overall visual appearance of HTML documents on the web. Without CSS, every website would look like a plain, unstyled document with no personality or visual hierarchy.

It separates content structure from presentation, allowing developers to create responsive, styled websites rather than plain text, enabling styling for screens, printing, or accessibility tools. With CSS, a developer can transform a simple HTML page into a visually stunning experience that works across all devices and screen sizes.

Between the Angle Brackets: The Brian Holt Scandal

Following the 2015 scandal involving Brian Williams' embellished reporting, Lester Holt was appointed as permanent anchor of NBC Nightly News after serving as interim anchor for four months. The transition was one of the most closely watched moments in broadcast journalism history.

Holt described his experience as an awkward situation with a firewall between him and network executives during the transition. Despite the unusual circumstances, he handled the role with professionalism and composure, quickly earning the trust of both viewers and colleagues across the network.

Scientist Invents New Miracle Drink: Coffee

Recent 2025 and 2026 research has highlighted coffee as a potent health-boosting beverage, with experts even referring to it as a miracle drink due to its wide-ranging protective effects against aging and disease.

Far from just a caffeine kick, new studies indicate that coffee acts at a cellular level to enhance longevity and wellness. Researchers from leading universities have found that regular coffee consumption is linked to a reduced risk of type 2 diabetes, Parkinson's disease, and certain types of cancer.

The antioxidants found in coffee, particularly chlorogenic acids, have been shown to reduce inflammation throughout the body. This anti-inflammatory effect is believed to be one of the key reasons why coffee drinkers tend to live longer and healthier lives compared to those who abstain.

Scientists have also discovered that coffee supports brain health by protecting neurons from degeneration. Regular consumption has been associated with improved memory, sharper focus, and a significantly lower risk of developing Alzheimer's disease in later life.

Despite decades of debate over its safety, the scientific consensus has shifted dramatically in coffee's favor. Health professionals who once urged caution are now acknowledging that two to four cups per day may be one of the simplest and most enjoyable habits a person can adopt for long-term health.

The findings have sent shockwaves through the wellness industry, with nutritionists and dietitians rushing to update their dietary guidelines. Coffee, it seems, is no longer just a morning ritual — it is fast becoming one of the most studied and celebrated health drinks of the modern era.
